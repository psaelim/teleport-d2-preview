Sign in & sign up

1 · Wrong code

Error state of the existing Verify screen, same route.

Enter the code.

We texted a 6-digit code to (555) 000-1234.

That code didn’t match. Two more tries, then we’ll text you a fresh one.

Codes expire after 10 minutes.

Behavior: the first keystroke clears all six boxes and the error. After the third miss the copy flips to “That code is used up. We texted you a new one.” and the old code dies — no lockout screen, the fresh code IS the recovery. Danger is text + a 2px ring on the boxes (#DE1600, 4.5:1); nothing turns coral, nothing shakes.

2 · Under-18 path

Shown when the DOB on Create account computes under 18.

Create account

This part needs a parent.

Teleport accounts belong to adults. Under 18, a parent or guardian books your care from their account — you’re one of their Family.

We text your parent or guardian
A link to sign in, or to create their own account
They add you under Family
Your name and birthday arrive prefilled — one tap
Care works the same
They book; you take the visit

Nothing you typed is saved until they approve. If the text never arrives, nothing exists.

Why this shape: it reuses Family instead of inventing a teen account type — the member they add is instantly bookable under “Who is this for?”, which already works. The escape hatch (“I mistyped my birthday”) returns to the form with fields intact. For review: DEV-FAQ open decision #9 (family consent) is still open — this drafts the product answer for it.

3 · Terms & privacy

Opens from the links on Sign in; also from You > Settings later.

Terms & privacy

The short version
You pay per visit. No subscription, no membership, no surprise line items.
Your records are yours. Download or delete them whenever you want.
We never sell your health data, and we never will.
Licensed clinicians make every medical call. The app just gets them to you.
Cancel any visit free up to 10 hours before it starts.

The short version is a summary, not the agreement. The full text below is the one that binds.

1. The service

Teleport MD arranges video visits with licensed providers and at-home sample collection by licensed clinical staff. Teleport MD is not itself a medical practice and does not employ your doctor…

2. Your account

One account per adult. You are responsible for keeping your phone number current, since sign-in codes and visit updates are sent by text…

3. Visits and cancellation

Visits may be cancelled without charge until 10 hours before the scheduled start…

Effective July 20, 2026 · Version 1.0

Acceptance stays passive — the “By continuing you agree” line on Sign in is the act; no checkbox, per the identity-only sign-up philosophy. The two links there become real routes (terms, one screen, two tabs). For review: the five short-version claims are product promises — legal needs to sign off that each one survives the real terms.

6 · Cancel this visit?

Mirrors the at-home Cancel ask; video has no nurse driving, so the stakes copy changes.

Cancel

Cancel this visit?

Wednesday’s 10:15 with Dr. Anand goes back to the practice, and your note to her is deleted.

Tell us why (optional)

Free to cancel — you’re well before the 10-hour mark.

V1 has no fees, so inside 10 hours the only change is the last line (“Inside 10 hours we still cancel — we just tell the practice you’re not coming.”). The $25 late-fee variant returns with pricing and goes into V2-FEATURES.md like the draw’s cost copy did. “Keep my visit” stays first and calm, same order as the draw cancel.

7 · Cancelled

The after-state; same cascade grammar as the draw cancel.

Cancelled

This visit is off.

Wednesday 10:15 AM with Dr. Anand is cancelled; the practice knows.

What happens now
Nothing was charged and nothing goes on your record. Dr. Anand stays on your care team, so booking again is the instant path — no new request.

Cascade: the Visits Upcoming row disappears, a Cancelled entry lands in Past, the reminder notification is withdrawn and replaced with “Visit cancelled” — identical plumbing to the draw’s state.cx cascade, driven by one flag.

Request declined by practice

8 · Practice can’t take you

The other ending of Request sent — opened from its notification.

Practice full

Dr. Okafor can’t take new patients right now.

The practice reviewed your Thursday request and their panel is full. That’s their capacity, not anything about you.

What happened to your request
Thursday 2:30 PM was released, and your note to Dr. Okafor was deleted without being read. Nothing was booked and nothing was shared beyond your name.

From here, search only shows doctors accepting new patients.

Copy stance: never “declined” or “rejected” at the patient — the pill states the reason (Practice full, gray = settled), the headline names the fact, and the blame lands explicitly on capacity. The privacy line (“note deleted unread”) is a real promise the backend must keep. For review: does the practice ever decline for another reason (out of network, wrong specialty)? If so the pill/headline need one variant per reason we’re willing to say out loud.
