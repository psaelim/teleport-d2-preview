The design system.

One system across the patient app and the provider portal: five colors, two typefaces, a two-weight icon set, and a small number of hard rules that every screen passes through an automated gate. Last updated July 20, 2026.

03 · Typography

Bricolage speaks, Inter works

Bricolage Grotesque for headlines, numbers and anything with a voice; Inter for UI. Nothing below 13px, body at 15 to 17px, weights 400 to 800.

Body · Inter 400-500 · 15pxA licensed nurse, at your door.

Eyebrow · Inter 600 · 13px capsThursday, July 17
Floor: 13px, and the scale is fixed: 13 · 14 · 15 · 17 · 20 · 22 · 30 · 40. No half-pixel sizes, no off-scale values; in Tailwind these are the redefined text-xs/sm/base/lg plus display classes. The gate rejects anything under 13px, including inline styles.

Builds as font-display = Bricolage Grotesque (headings, numbers), font-sans = Inter. text-xs is redefined to 13px so the floor holds; arbitrary values below it are a build error.

04 · Buttons

One blue action per page

Capsule shape, 44px minimum height. The hierarchy is positional: electric is the page's one global action, ink acts on the card it sits in, ghost is secondary.

On light surfaces

Cancel this visit

Electric = page action (header). Ink = card action. Ghost = secondary. Destructive is quiet danger text, never a filled red button.

On ink surfaces

On dark cards the electric primary returns, paired with a white-outline ghost. Volt buttons carry ink labels only.

Builds as shadcn Button, rounded-full, sizes redefined to h-11 (44px) / h-12. Variants: default = electric (one per page), secondary = ink (card actions), outline = ghost (1px border-input), destructive = restyled to quiet danger text, never a filled red button.

05 · Status

Chips: tint plus ink, never loud

Every status is a pale tint of an accent carrying ink text. The grammar is fixed; a developer should be able to pick the color from the state alone.

Crew en routeEn routeDraw en routeBlue #DCE4FF — in motion right now
Needs confirmationHighCoral #FFDDD8 — needs a human, or out of range
UnconfirmedFinding crewRequestedGray #E8E8E3 — idle, waiting, nothing to do yet

Builds as shadcn Badge replaced by a StatusChip with exactly four variants (green/blue/coral/gray), ink text, rounded-full min-h-7. The state-to-variant mapping is fixed; never choose a chip color by taste.

The status machine speaks plain language. Requested → Confirmed → Accepted → En route → Arrived → Collected → Dropped off. The old labels (#WO1, "Pending Crew") are banned on every user-facing surface.

06 · Cards & surfaces

White on paper, one ink anchor

Surfaces are white cards with hairline borders on the paper canvas, radius 16. Each page gets at most one ink hero card as its anchor, and coral edges mark the one card that needs you.

08 · Forms

One field at a time

No funnels. Sign-up is identity only; everything else is captured at the moment it is needed, one field per screen on mobile. Inputs are 48px, radius 12, with the label above, never a placeholder-as-label.

Search

Search is one box that accepts anything (name, specialty, clinic). The seven-field provider lookup is gone and stays gone.

Builds as shadcn Input at h-12 (48px) with Label above; Checkbox, RadioGroup, Select as shipped, restyled by the theme. Validation text uses text-destructive.

09 · Voice

Plain language is a component

The jargon sweep is part of the build gate: these words never reach a user-facing surface. Say what is happening, honestly, including waiting.

10 · Photography

Real people, calm rooms

Do

  • Real photography: patients at home, clinicians in daylight
  • Warm, calm interiors; the brand accent may appear in the scene
  • Faces engaged with the moment, not the camera (except portraits)
  • Generated imagery is screened: no lettering, no logos, no fake UI on visible screens

Don't

  • No cartoon or friendly-blob illustration, anywhere (standing team rule)
  • No stock-photo gloss: fake smiles, white voids, pointing at screens
  • No visible phone or laptop screens with readable UI
  • No hourglasses, no spinners as personality

11 · Interaction

States are specified, not inherited

Radix and shadcn will render focus, hover, and disabled states whether or not anyone designed them. These are the designed ones; do not let library defaults leak through.

Focus

Keyboard focus: 2px electric ring, 3px offset (ring-2 ring-ring ring-offset-2). On ink surfaces the ring is volt. Focus is never removed, only styled; hash navigation moves focus to the new page's heading.

Hover & press

Electric buttons deepen to --electric-hover #093FFF · ink buttons go #000 · ghost borders darken to ink · list rows tint to #FAFAF7 · cards that navigate lift 2 to 3px with a soft shadow · press scales .99. Color shifts, never opacity fades.

Disabled

45% opacity, cursor default, still readable. A disabled control always says why nearby ("Coming soon", "Saving…"); silent dead buttons are a defect.

Loading

Skeletons in --chip-gray shaped like the content (shadcn Skeleton). In-button loading = label swaps to a present-tense verb ("Confirming…"). No full-screen spinners; waiting always comes with words and, where possible, a time expectation.

Respect prefers-reduced-motion: lifts, scales and skeleton shimmer collapse to instant state changes.

12 · Accessibility

The gate is the spec

Every screen in both apps passes an automated audit before it ships. The checks target exactly what the old app failed.

44px

Minimum interactive target, both axes. Measured on rendered layout, not design intent.

AA

4.5:1 body, 3:1 large text, on the real composited background. The palette rules above exist because of this check.

13px

Type floor. Nothing smaller renders, including inline styles and generated content.

audit.js runs the gate per route in the page console and is written to port to CI. Current status: 0 failures across all 43 shipped screens (38 patient + 5 provider), plus a clean jargon sweep.
